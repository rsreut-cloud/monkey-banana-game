# 🐵🍌 Feed the Monkeys

This one's an experiment, not a "real" project — and it's my first ever
build with [Claude Code](https://claude.com/claude-code).

**Live demo:** https://monkey-banana-game-alpha.vercel.app

## Where it came from

The idea belongs to my four-year-old nephew. He wanted a game where you
feed monkeys bananas — so I built it, right then, with him sitting on my
lap watching the screen. The whole thing came together in just a few
minutes, and later got one short follow-up session of small fixes
(a mobile-friendly layout, a sound picker, different arrangement modes).

## Why I'm proud of it

There's nothing complicated here — just monkeys, bananas, and a kid's
idea turned into something that actually runs in a browser and that he
can play. That's exactly the point. This was a small, playful test of
how fast an idea can become something real, and I love how simple it
stayed.

## How it came together, from where I was sitting

Watching this get built was honestly the fun part — I'd describe what my
nephew wanted between distractions (he kept grabbing the mouse), and
within minutes there were monkeys on screen reacting to dragged bananas.
No planning documents, no back-and-forth about architecture — just a
plain HTML/CSS/JS page, one clear idea, and a few fast iterations until
it felt right for a four-year-old to play. It's a good reminder that not
every build needs to be a "project" — sometimes it's just an idea and a
kid on your lap, and that's enough.

## What's actually in here

- Plain HTML/CSS/JavaScript, no framework — the right size for a game
  this small.
- Built in one short burst with Claude Code.
- One later session added mobile-friendly layout, a sound picker, and a
  couple of monkey-arrangement modes.
